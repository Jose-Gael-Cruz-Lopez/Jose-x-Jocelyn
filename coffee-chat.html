Section 01

How this network works

No guessing. No cold-messaging strangers. Just a directory of people who said yes.

Everyone in this directory has filled out a short form and agreed to be listed as a connection. That means you are not guessing if someone is open to messages — you already know they are. The goal is to make networking feel less random and more like a structured, human directory you can navigate by interest, role, identity, and location.

You pick who to reach out to, you send the request directly (usually via LinkedIn), and you schedule time that works for both of you. Jose and Jocelyn are not brokering introductions in the middle — this is a self-serve network designed to scale and stay lightweight.

Step 01

Browse the directory

Use the search bar and filters to find people whose backgrounds, roles, or paths align with where you are trying to go. Read their cards carefully before reaching out.

Step 02

Read their profile

Every card shows their current role, their path, what topics they can talk about, and how many chats per month they can take. Use all of that before you write your message.

Step 03

Send a personalized message

Use our coffee chat request templates as a starting point. Keep it short, specific, and respectful of their time. Under 80 words is a good rule of thumb.

Step 04

Show up prepared

If they accept, come ready with 3–5 real questions. Do your research. Be on time. Send a thank-you after. These small things matter more than most people think.

Section 03

How to reach out (without being awkward)

A strong first message is short, specific, and human.

What makes a good message

Before you message anyone in the network, read their card and only reach out if something about their path actually connects to where you are trying to go. Your first message should be under 80 words, mention something specific about their background, and ask for a simple 15–20 minute chat — not "can you mentor me forever?"

A strong message usually includes three things: who you are, why you are reaching out to them specifically, and a clear, respectful ask. The Career Templates page has ready-to-use coffee chat request templates you can copy and personalize for each person you contact.

See coffee chat templates

The do / don't list

✓ Do read their profile and LinkedIn before messaging.
✓ Do personalize at least one sentence to their specific background.
✓ Do show up on time and prepared with real questions.
✓ Do send a short thank-you within 24 hours.
✕ Don't ask them to get you a job or a referral in the first message.
✕ Don't send copy-paste messages to ten people in one hour.
✕ Don't ask questions you could have Googled in 30 seconds.
✕ Don't ghost after they accept — that harms everyone in the network.
