# Impeccable Design Context — Jose x Jocelyn / From Campus to Career

## Design Context

### Users
First-generation and underrepresented students in tech and data — actively job-searching, mid-internship-hunt, or early in their first role. They arrive in a state of **structured uncertainty**: they don't know what they don't know. The dominant emotional state is the quiet shame of not having palancas, not having parents who've done this, and not knowing if they're doing it wrong. They use this site during the job search — late nights, browser tabs stacked with job postings, before a coffee chat they don't know how to prepare for.

**Job to be done**: Find people two steps ahead who actually want them to catch up — and get the tactical, honest, no-marketing-speak answers they can't get from their university career center.

### Brand Personality
**Real. Warm. Grounded.**

- **Real**: The piñata break messages are written in English + Spanish, in confessional voice, and are buried behind 7 clicks. That is the most brand-defining decision on the site — it tells you who this is for without stating it. A corporation would never do this. The layered photo hover (professional → truer self) encodes the same idea: the real person is underneath the public face.
- **Warm**: Terra cotta + cream + gold on a dark body is a Mexican earthenware palette. --color-cream: #F2E4CE as the page base (not white) is the single most important warmth decision. Clash Display + Satoshi mirrors the J&J relationship: heavy + precise on top, human + readable underneath.
- **Grounded**: No gradients in the palette. Ease-out easing family throughout (power2.out, power3.out, cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)) — things land and stop, they don't overshoot. No trend-chasing.

### Emotional Goal
**"I finally found people who are two steps ahead of me and actually want me to catch up."**

The specific cocktail: **specific recognition + forward momentum + permission to not know things.**

The site delivers recognition and momentum well. It under-delivers on *permission to not know things*. The most powerful design move available is to have the first text below the fold name the student's fear before offering anything. Lead with their shame, not your identity.

The piñata messages prove the team knows how to write this way:
> "No palancas needed. Just you and this community."
> "First-gen is not a limitation. It's the origin story."

All copy should match that register. Never: "empowering underserved communities." Always: direct address to the student's actual fear.

### Anti-References

**1. Corporate LinkedIn / LinkedIn Learning**
The visual danger: navy + white + rounded badge cards + statistics. The specific at-risk component is `.c2c__card` (white background, colored top border, rounded corners, soft shadow on a cream page). This exact pattern is Tailwind UI's feature card. It reads as LinkedIn Learning. The c2c__card-number in Playfair italic gold fights to save it but the container wins.

**2. Bootcamp Landing Pages** (Lambda School, Springboard, Flatiron)
Tells: countdown timers, "X students enrolled this week," outcome statistics in large numbers on accent blocks, CTA buttons that say "Apply Now." The multi-step modal with progress dots is the component most at risk of reading as a funnel. The copy inside each step is what separates invitation from pressure.

**3. Nonprofit Charity Aesthetic** (Idealist, DoSomething)
Tells: centered layout, large soft illustrations, pastel backgrounds, "bridging the gap" copy, stock photos of diverse students smiling at laptops. The palette and typography already prevent this. The only drift risk is in About section closing copy — if it sounds like a grant application, it breaks the brand.

**4. Generic Aesthetic Career Creator** (Instagram Canva-slide accounts)
Tells: uniform carousel aesthetics, motivational quote formats, Cormorant Garamond italic or thin sans-serif, peach-beige. Clash Display and terracotta already prevent this. The LinkedIn Series page is the most at risk if content format becomes predictable.

### Design Principles

1. **Lead with their fear, not your identity.** Every section's first text should name what the student is feeling before it offers what J&J does about it. The hero is the one exception — it's the brand mark, and it earns its right to be an identity statement via the piñata's confessional layer underneath.

2. **Earn every white surface.** The site's base is cream, not white. White is a deliberate step up — it signals something clean, open, or premium. Do not use white backgrounds for utility cards. Use colored surface blocks (teal, terracotta, gold, navy) or cream. White + rounded border + shadow = SaaS template. Avoid it.

3. **Texture earns its opacity.** The services panel diagonal-line pattern at 4% is invisible on most monitors. Any decorative texture must be visible without straining. If a texture doesn't read at casual glance, remove it or increase its opacity to at least 8%.

4. **Interior pages carry the brand, not just the homepage.** Sub-pages are often the first impression for someone arriving from LinkedIn or a search result. They don't see the loader, the hero, or the piñata. The brand's kinetic energy and personality must exist on every page — not just index.html. Any new component designed on the homepage should be backported or adapted for sub-page contexts.

5. **End with personality.** The footer's rotated gold CTA is the best single component on the site. The site should end with the same energy it carries throughout — a 13px muted credit line is not a closing beat. Every page should have a final emotional moment before the footer legal text.

### Established Visual System

**Palette (non-negotiable)**
- Terracotta accent: `#B34539` / hover `#963A30`
- Gold: `#E8A838` / hero gold `#f5c026`
- Teal: `#3A7D6B`
- Blue: `#5B8EC2`
- Navy: `#162B44` / light `#1E3A58`
- Cream base: `#F2E4CE`
- Dark body: `#1A1916`
- Muted text: `#8A7E72` ⚠️ **fails WCAG AA at small sizes on cream** — darken to ~`#6B5E52` for body text

**Typography (non-negotiable)**
- Display: `Clash Display` (heavy, geometric, editorial)
- Body: `Satoshi` (humanist, clear)
- Serif accent: `Playfair Display` (italic numbers, decorative pull-quotes)
- Hero names: `Montserrat` (geometric caps, wide tracking)
- Do not introduce new fonts without replacing one of the above

**Motion language**
- Easing family: `power2.out`, `power3.out`, `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` — always ease-out, never elastic or bounce
- Entry animations: opacity + translateY, staggered by 0.08–0.12s increments
- All motion gated by `prefers-reduced-motion` check in both CSS (7 declarations) and JS

**Established interaction patterns**
- Loader: 3 gold bands wipe open (left-right-left)
- Nav: fixed top-right, transparent over hero (white links), dark over light sections
- Tabs: underline active with gold, no background active state
- Cards: full-color surface blocks, not white-on-white containers
- Buttons: outlined with border, filled on hover (translateY(-2px))
- Hover photos: professional → real (opacity cross-fade)

### Known Accessibility Gaps (fix before shipping new components)

| Issue | Location | Fix |
|-------|----------|-----|
| Contrast fail (3.1:1) | `--color-muted` on cream, small text | Darken to `#6B5E52` |
| Touch target 32px | `.about__card-social` (LinkedIn/IG) | Add `padding: 6px`, adjust inner image |
| `aria-hidden` missing | Gallery cards at `opacity: 0` | Set `aria-hidden="true"` dynamically when slot ≥ 5 |
| Missing `:focus-visible` | Nav links | Add gold outline on hero, dark on cream |
| Missing `aria-label` + `aria-expanded` | Mobile burger button | Toggle both on click |
| Piñata not keyboard accessible | `.pinata__body` is a `div` | Convert to `<button>` |
| `aria-hidden` missing | `spawnHitStars()` confetti | Add `star.setAttribute('aria-hidden', 'true')` |
